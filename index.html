Common Questions

What landlords ask before they qualify.

If there's no interest, how do you make money?

We recover our investment through the monthly payments your new tenant's rent funds — the same way tenant-improvement allowances are commonly structured across commercial real estate. There's no rate hidden in a spread; the cost of the work is simply split into equal payments over your chosen term (24, 36, or 60 months).

What happens if I sell or refinance before the term ends?

The remaining balance becomes due in full at closing — a standard balloon structure used throughout commercial real estate financing, not unique to us. We coordinate directly with your title company so it's settled as part of the transaction.

What if my tenant defaults or leaves early?

Qualification is based on your property, the scope of work, and a signed construction agreement — not a single tenant's lease. We'll walk through exactly how this is handled for your specific property during your consultation, in writing, before any work begins.

What kind of properties qualify?

We focus specifically on shopping center/retail units and rehabilitation center build-outs. In general, properties need a signed or pending lease, a clear scope of work, and rental income sufficient to support the monthly payment.

Why finance the build-out instead of paying for it myself?

A generous, ready-to-build space is one of the strongest levers landlords have for attracting higher-quality, longer-term tenants — but funding it out of pocket ties up capital you could use elsewhere. Financing the build-out lets you compete for the tenant you want without touching your cash reserves.
